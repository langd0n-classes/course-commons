# GenAI Explorations (GAIEs)

## What they are

A **GAIE** (GenAI-Integrated Exercise, also called a GenAI Exploration) is a
pre-class, out-of-class activity where students use a GenAI tool to encounter
new concepts *before* the instructor introduces them in lecture.

The student's first contact with the material is through guided AI interaction,
not through reading or watching a video. The lecture then consolidates from a
position of prior exposure rather than starting from zero.

## Key characteristics

- **Out of class** — completed before the associated lecture
- **Completion-graded** — not correctness-graded; the goal is exposure and
  productive struggle, not right answers
- **Scoped and guided** — students work through a specific set of concepts or
  problems; they are not just "ask the AI anything"
- **Feeds the lecture** — the lecture assumes students have done the GAIE and
  can build on it
- **GenAI as interlocutor, not answer machine** — students are expected to
  probe, question, and verify outputs, modeling the verification behavior the
  course teaches

## Placement in the learning arc

GAIEs are the **Introduce** phase (I) of the I → P → A skill progression.
They are the first touch of a skill before it is Practiced in class and
Assessed in a graded artifact.

In ds100's weekly rhythm: **GAIE (out of class) → Tuesday Consolidation →
Thursday Assessment → Friday Lab**

## Course-specific notes

**DS-100:** GAIEs use TerrierGPT (BU's hosted GenAI tool). Numbered as
`gaie-##-slug`, e.g., `gaie-01-programming-concepts`. One GAIE per module,
due before the first lecture of each module.

**DS-551:** GAIEs use TerrierGPT or equivalent (students may use other tools;
syllabus notes ~$60 cost for paid tools if needed). Numbered `gaie-##-slug`
where `##` matches the lecture the GAIE precedes. Not every lecture has a
GAIE — coverage is selective. GAIE files live in
`course-content/gaies/gaie-##-slug/`. The G/I/P/A coverage arc is tracked in
`course-info/term/ds551-topics-tracker.md`.

## Naming convention

`gaie-{##}-{short-slug}.md` — follows the standard `{type}-{##}-{slug}` pattern.
See `naming-conventions.md`.
